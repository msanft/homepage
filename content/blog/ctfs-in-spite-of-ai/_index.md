Quite exactly 10 years before writing this blog post, in March of 2016,
AlphaGo beat Lee Sedol, one of the best Go players in history.[^1]
It was the first time for an AI system to beat a player of Sedol's
rank, and it caused quite an uproar in both the Go and AI community.[^2]

After a career of over 20 years, Sedol retired in 2019 over the
increasing dominance of AI systems in the competitive Go community.[^3]

It would be megalomaniac to compare myself to someone like Sedol, but
with the recent influence of AI systems on competitive CTFs, I claim
to have felt just a fraction of what Sedol must've felt after being
beaten by AlphaGo.

> are, so now would probably be a good time to fire up a good ol'

## The Status Quo

During the last year, AI became significantly more prominent in the CTF
community. Conventionally, CTF organizers provide some sort of channel
for players to discuss their solutions to challenges post-CTF. Some
time last year, I started to see posts like "I just dropped everything
into Claude / ChatGPT / ...".

The rate of these posts increased, and it still does. People are using
AI to solve CTF challenges more and more.

My first response to these posts was anger. Either I was angry to not
have tried to stuff the challenge into some AI system myself, losing my
team valuable points on the scoreboard, or I was angry to have spent
hours to solve some challenge that was solved within mere seconds of
active effort by a player using AI.

Responses to these developments from the general CTF community are -who
would have guessed- mixed. The CTF community is small, but at the same
time large and diverse enough to obviously have different perspectives
and thus, opinions on the topic. We have the average AI maximalists
that can't help themselves but tell everyone how old-fashioned it is to
actually press keys on your keyboard while participating in CTFs, but
also the average AI doomsayers pronouncing CTFs dead already. As so
often in today's very polarized world, I think the answer is somewhere
in the middle of these two.
